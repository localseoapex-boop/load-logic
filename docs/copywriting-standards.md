# Copywriting Standards

## Purpose

These standards apply to every page on this website including:

- Homepage
- Service pages
- Service area pages
- Blog posts
- FAQs
- Meta titles
- Meta descriptions
- CTAs
- Landing pages

Every piece of copy should follow these rules.

---

## Writing Style

Write like an experienced human copywriter for a local home service company.

The copy should feel natural, trustworthy, and helpful.

It should sound like a skilled local business owner or professional copywriter wrote it.

Never sound like AI.

---

## Never Use

- Em dashes (—)
- En dashes (–)
- Semicolons (;)
- AI clichés
- Corporate buzzwords
- Generic marketing language
- Empty filler

Avoid phrases like:

- Industry-leading
- Cutting-edge
- Game-changing
- Unlock
- Revolutionize
- Seamless
- Comprehensive solutions
- Best-in-class
- State-of-the-art

---

## Reading Level

Write at approximately a 7th-9th grade reading level.

Keep sentences easy to read.

Use contractions naturally.

Vary sentence length.

Prefer short paragraphs.

---

## Content Quality

Every sentence must communicate useful information.

Remove any sentence that exists only to sound impressive.

Show instead of tell.

Explain benefits instead of making unsupported claims.

Avoid repeating ideas.

Get to the point quickly.

---

## Local SEO

Write for homeowners first.

Search engines second.

Mention the target city naturally.

Do not keyword stuff.

Use natural language.

---

## Trust

Never invent:

- awards
- certifications
- years in business
- statistics
- guarantees

Only state facts that are known.

---

## Human Review

Before considering copy complete:

Read every sentence as if you were a homeowner.

If any sentence sounds AI-generated, rewrite it.

If any sentence sounds overly polished, simplify it.

If any sentence could have been written by any company, rewrite it so it sounds local and authentic.

Every page should feel like it was written by an experienced human copywriter.
