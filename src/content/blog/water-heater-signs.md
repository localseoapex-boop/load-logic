A failing water heater rarely dies without warning. Catch these signs early and
you can plan a replacement instead of facing a cold-shower emergency.

## 1. Rusty or discolored hot water

Brown or metallic-tasting hot water often means the tank is corroding from the inside.

## 2. Rumbling or popping noises

Sediment buildup makes the tank work harder and shortens its lifespan.

## 3. Water pooling around the base

Any moisture near the tank signals a leak that usually can't be repaired.

## 4. It's over 10 years old

Most tank heaters last 8–12 years. Past that, efficiency drops fast.

## 5. Not enough hot water

If you're running out sooner than you used to, the tank may be failing.
